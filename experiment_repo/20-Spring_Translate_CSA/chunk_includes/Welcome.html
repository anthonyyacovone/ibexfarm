Thank you for participating in this study!


In this study, you will be asked to rate Spanish translations of English words. First, you will see sentences like "Is LEONA an acceptable translation of CAT?" For this study, "acceptable" means that any native Spanish speaker could use this word to translate the English word on the screen.

To provide your rating, you will press "f" for "Acceptable" and "j" for "Not acceptable". Then, you will be asked "How confident you are in your acceptability rating of that last translation?" on a scale from 1-7. Finally, we ask if you knew both of the words presented (as some of them are a bit strange!).

What if I don't know the Spanish or the English word(s) in the sentence?

  • Go with your best guess on the acceptability rating and then rate your confidence as extremely low (e.g. 1 or 2).
  • After rating, you will be asked whether you knew both words or just one of them!

    • What if I never use this Spanish word personally but I know others do/could use it?

  • Your ratings should reflect whether any native speaker could reasonably use this Spanish word as a translation.
  • So, your ratings should not based on what you would say personally, but rather what any Spanish speaker could say!.
