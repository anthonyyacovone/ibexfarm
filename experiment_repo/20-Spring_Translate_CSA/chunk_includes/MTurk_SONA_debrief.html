DEBRIEF SHEET

Thank you for participating in this study!

What is this study about?

This study is part of a larger study looking at how bilingual speakers comprehend "code-switches" or shifting between languages during comprehension. We previously monitored participants' brain responses to the words that you translated in this study. The words originally occurred in a larger story context, but we wanted to ensure that our translations were adequate. You may have noticed that some translations were awful, others excellent, and others in the middle. We are looking for how our translations were rated by you and other Spanish-English bilinguals at Harvard.

How did we do this?

We took all of the "target" words from our study and paired them with our "natural" translations or our "unnatural" translations. We hope to see that the natural translations are rated as acceptable more often than the "unnatural" ones. Consider the example comparisons below:

  • Is sol an acceptable translation of sun. (acceptable translation)
  • Is luz an acceptable translation of sun. (relatively unacceptable translation)

    • We were interested in a few things given these comparisons:
     1) Did our intuitions match those of the population of bilingual speakers at Harvard?
      2) Did our original translations actually match the intended word in English?

    Why should we do this study?

    This study helps us better understand the results that we found in our earlier study. We are trying to ensure that student participants agree with our intuitions and know the words from the study!
